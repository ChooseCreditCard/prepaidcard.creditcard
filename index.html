What is a prepaid card?

A prepaid card is a payment card that you load with money before using it. You’re spending your own balance, not borrowing from a credit line.

  • • Load funds by bank transfer, cash, or another card
  • • Spend online or in-store like a normal card
  • • When the balance hits zero, transactions are declined

Who are prepaid cards for?

Budget control

Great for people who want strict limits and no risk of overspending into debt.

Teens & students

Parents can load money for travel, school or online purchases with clear limits.

Travel use

Some prepaid cards are built for travel with multi-currency wallets and FX benefits.

Online safety

Prepaid cards can isolate risky or one-off online purchases from your main account.

Pros and cons vs. credit cards

Prepaid cards behave differently from traditional credit cards:

  • • No credit line: you can’t go into card debt
  • • No credit building: usage usually doesn’t build your credit score
  • • Fewer perks: travel insurance and lounge access are rare
  • • Often simpler KYC: but still regulated and verified
